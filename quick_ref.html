Set Up

  1. Place a metal planet in the inner ring, the target plannet in the second right, a gas planet and a metal planet, or two gas planets in the third ring, and two ice planets in the outer ring.
  2. Place the colony ship and player ships on the outer ring space where the colony ship orbit transitions to the third ring.
  3. Give each player a ship card, tool token and an action indicator token; place the ship mat small ship side up and place the action indicator face up on the ship card.
  4. Place enough fuel tokens on each ship card to fill all of the spaces.
  5. Start the round timer (if using) and begin play

Player Turns

Players may take their turns each round in any order. During a turn each player may take one of the following actions:

  • Move
  • Retool their ship (for mining or building )
  • Mine a resource (if tooled for mining and at a resource planet)
  • Build a structure (if tooled for building* and carying sufficient resources)
  • Upgrade a ship (if tooled for building* and colocated with the ship being upgraded). Only an upgraded ship () can transport water
They may also do the following at any time:
  • Re-fuel (if at a station with a fuel module)
  • Transfer fuel or resources between colocated ships
Players may also roll the dice once to try to gain a bonus build or mine action. The roll can have the following outcomes:
  • 1 or 2 - Their tool breaks and they will need to retool before building or mining again.
  • 3 or 4 - The attempt fails
  • 5 or 6 - The attempt succeeds and the player can proceed with the extra action

Players indicate the have used their action (or are choosing not to) by turning their action token face down. When all action tokens are face down, everything orbits one space counter clockwise, players flip their action tokens up again, the round timer is restarted (if using), and a new round begins.

* When at a station equiped with an engineering module players may perform build actions reguardless of thier ship tooling.

Special events

At the beginning of a round, when the timer is started the following special events may occur:

  • Extreme solar activity - players may not communicate with each other during the round (no talking about the game or pointing)
  • Time Dialation - the timer for this round is shorter than normal
If the timer expires before all players have turned their action tokens face down one of the following happens to a random player
  • Tool breakage - the player's mining or build tool breaks and they must retool before using it
  • Fuel leak - the player's ship experiences a fuel leak and loses one fuel

Winning or Losing

Winning

Players win by collecting a sufficient amount of ice () and transporting it to the target planet. The amound needed should be chosen at the beginning of the game based on desired difficulty. One or two per player is recommend.

Losing

The players lose if the colony ship reaches the target planet before sufficient ice is delivered.

Difficulty

There are several ways to adjust the difficulty aside from the amount of ice required to win. The round timer can be adjusted longer or shorter (for a more frantic game) or not used at all (for beginners or those who don't like time pressure). The number of resource planets in play can also be changed (use only one of each type for maximum challenge). The target planet can also be placed in the third orbit rather than the second to shorted the number of turns before the colony ship arrives
